When to get it checked

These are the warning signs owners usually notice first

If the engine sounds rough on startup or the car no longer feels right, it is worth getting the timing system checked sooner rather than later.

Common signs

  • Rattle on cold start
  • Restricted Performance message
  • P0016 or P0017 fault codes
  • Loss of power or poor running
  • Timing correlation faults

What your repair includes

This service is built for owners who care about quality. The focus is not speed for speed’s sake. The focus is getting the job done properly and giving you confidence in what has been done.

1

Official JLR diagnostic checks

Your vehicle is checked with official JLR software to confirm timing-related issues and verify phase positions as part of the process.

2

Careful strip-down and refit

The job is carried out methodically with proper attention to detail. This is designed for owners who want more care and more focus than the rushed feel many associate with the main dealer approach.

3

No shortcuts on sealing and reassembly

Components are properly sealed and refitted, with time allowed for everything to cure and dry as it should before final testing and handover.

4

Clean assembly standards

The sump is blocked during cleaning to reduce the risk of debris or excess sealant entering the oil system. Small details matter because small contamination can create big problems later.

5

Yellow Mark quality control

Critical fasteners are torqued to factory specification and witness-marked in yellow, giving visible proof of careful assembly and quality control.

6

Photo updates and full transparency

Pictures are taken throughout the repair and sent to you, so you can see exactly what has been done, how it has been done, and what your vehicle looks like during the process.

Frequently asked questions

Short answers to the things most owners want to know before picking up the phone.

What does the repair cost?

Jaguar models listed on this page are £1,599. Range Rover models listed on this page are £1,899.

How long does the repair take?

Typical turnaround is 3 to 5 working days. That allows enough time for proper strip-down, sealing, drying, reassembly, testing and final checks before the vehicle is returned.

Does the repair come with a warranty?

Yes. Your repair includes a warranty on parts and workmanship for 2 years or 10,000 miles, whichever comes first. The warranty is non-transferable and applies only to the original purchaser of the service.

Will I be able to see what has been done?

Yes. Pictures are taken during the process and sent to you, so you can see the work as it is carried out rather than being left guessing.

My car has P0016 or P0017. Is that relevant?

Yes. Those codes can point to timing correlation issues and are one of the common reasons owners start looking into the timing chain system.
